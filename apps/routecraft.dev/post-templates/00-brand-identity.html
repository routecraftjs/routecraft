III Typography

Editorial display. Technical voice.

Fraunces variable for everything literary. JetBrains Mono for everything technical. No third typeface.

Fraunces variable · opsz 9–144 · weight 200–900 · italic
Display · opsz 144 The shape of a sentence.
Subhead · opsz 96 Subheadings carry the rhythm.
Editorial · opsz 72 Italic captions, asides, callouts. Reads warm against the page.
Body · opsz 9 Long-form body and prose. Fraunces handles small sizes through its lowest optical setting; the strokes thicken to read clean at 16–18px.

Italic

The cobalt italic is the most identifying move. Use it on one phrase per heading, never on a whole sentence.

IV Voice

Editorial. Blueprint. Concrete.

Routecraft writes like an engineering broadsheet, not a SaaS landing page. Short clauses. Named things. No abstractions where a concrete noun fits.

Do

  • "Tools for agents. Or the whole agent."

    Declarative · names two real modes

  • "Same body. Different trigger. Change one line."

    Rhythmic · technical · provable

  • "Hands, not keys."

    Metaphor · stakes implied

  • "The boring infrastructure is already shipped."

    Honest about what we did

Don't

  • "Revolutionise your AI workflow!"

    Salesy · no proof

  • "The world's best automation framework"

    Empty superlative

  • "A synergistic enterprise solution for AI orchestration"

    Corporate vocabulary

  • "Unlock the power of agents — seamlessly."

    Generic landing-page filler

No em-dashes. Use ", " or a full stop.

Lowercase verbs in code: craft(), authorize(), guard().

Capabilities, not "tools" or "agents", as the framework noun.

Stake the claim with a named primitive, not adjectives.
